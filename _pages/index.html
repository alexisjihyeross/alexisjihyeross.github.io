--- layout: single permalink: / author_profile: true title: About Me description: "Alexis Ross's Website" --- I'm a senior at Harvard University studying Computer Science and Philosophy. My broad research interests are natural language processing and interpretable/explainable machine learning. In particular, I'm interested in understanding the kinds of knowledge represented in complex machine learning models and in developing methods to make such models more understandable to humans.

I can be reached via email at alexis_ross [at] college_harvard [dot] edu.

My Experience

  • I am currently writing a senior thesis in Computer Science and Philosophy on local counterfactual explanations for blackbox classifiers, advised by Hima Lakkaraju and Bernhard Nickel. My thesis aims to build explanations which preserve causal relationships between variables and classifier predictions.
  • Summer 2019: I was a research intern at Microsoft Research's Healthcare NeXT on Project Hanover, where I worked with Tristan Naumann and Hoifung Poon on biomedical machine reading for precision medicine.
  • Summer 2018: I was a member of the Sentence Representation Learning Team led by Ellie Pavlick and Sam Bowman at JSALT 2018.
