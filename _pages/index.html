--- layout: single permalink: / author_profile: true title: "About Me" description: "Alexis Ross's Website" --- Hi, I'm Alexis! I'm a recent graduate from Harvard, where I studied Computer Science and Philosophy. Currently, I'm a Predoctoral Young Investigator on the AllenNLP team at the Allen Institute for Artificial Intelligence (AI2) working with Matt Peters and Ana Marasovic. My research interests include explainability and interpretability in machine learning--I'm interested in studying what kinds of language understanding existing models do and do not have, as well as in developing methods to help us understand how they make their predictions.

I can be reached via email at alexisr [at] allenai [dot] org.

My Experiences

  • May 2020: I wrote my senior thesis in Computer Science and Philosophy on using local linear approximations as causal explanations of blackbox classification decisions. I was advised by Hima Lakkaraju and Bernhard Nickel.
  • Summer 2019: I was a research intern at Microsoft Research working on Project Hanover with Tristan Naumann and Hoifung Poon on biomedical machine reading for precision medicine.
  • Summer 2018: I was a member of the Sentence Representation Learning Team led by Ellie Pavlick and Sam Bowman at JSALT 2018.
  • Summer 2017: I was a research assistant in the Cognitive Neuropsychology Lab at Harvard, where I worked on object processing with Gilles Vannuscorps and Alfonso Caramazza.
