--- layout: single classes: wide permalink: / author_profile: true title: "About Me" description: "Alexis Ross's Website" --- Hi, I'm Alexis! I am a predoctoral researcher (PYI) on the AllenNLP team at the Allen Institute for Artificial Intelligence (AI2) working with Matthew Peters and Ana Marasovic. I am broadly interested in endowing models with the ability to understand and reason about language in human-like ways. My current focus is improving and evaluating the robustness of NLP models, particularly using explanations. I am also a frequent host of the NLP Highlights Podcast.

I graduated from Harvard in 2020 with a joint B.A. degree in Computer Science and Philosophy. During my undergrad, I wrote a senior thesis on causal explanations of machine learning models, advised by Hima Lakkaraju and Bernhard Nickel. I have had the privilege of interning at Microsoft Research, where I worked with Tristan Naumann and the rest of the Project Hanover team on biomedical machine reading for precision medicine. I have also been fortunate to have worked closely with Ellie Pavlick and the rest of the Sentence Representation Learning Team at JSALT 2018.

I can be reached via email at alexisr [at] allenai [dot] org.
